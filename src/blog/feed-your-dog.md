Walk down any pet-food aisle and the choices are overwhelming — grain-free, raw, breed-specific, "human-grade." The good news: feeding your dog well is simpler than the marketing makes it look. Here's what actually matters.

## Start with portion size

Most overweight dogs aren't eating bad food — they're eating too much of a good one. Use the feeding guide on the bag as a *starting point*, then adjust to your dog's body condition: you should be able to feel the ribs easily, and see a visible waist from above. When in doubt, measure with a proper cup rather than eyeballing it.

## Read the label, not the front of the bag

The front is advertising; the truth is on the back. Look for a named protein ("chicken," not "meat") in the first few ingredients, and a statement that the food is "complete and balanced" for your dog's life stage. That single line means the food meets established nutritional standards.

## Treats count too

Treats should make up no more than about **10%** of daily calories. Everything above that is coming out of a balanced diet. Swap some commercial treats for small pieces of carrot, green beans, or plain cooked chicken.

## When to ask your vet

Puppies, seniors, and dogs with conditions like kidney disease or allergies have different needs — and that's exactly the conversation to have at your next visit. A two-minute chat can save months of guessing.
