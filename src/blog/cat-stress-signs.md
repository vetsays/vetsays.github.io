Cats are masters at hiding discomfort — a survival instinct that makes stress easy to miss. These are the signals I tell owners to watch for.

## 1. Over-grooming

A stressed cat may lick one spot until the fur thins or disappears, often on the belly or legs. Skin problems can look similar, so it's worth a vet check to rule out allergies or pain.

## 2. Changes around the litter box

Going *outside* the box, or straining and visiting it often, can be behavioral **or** medical. Urinary issues in cats can become emergencies quickly, so never ignore a sudden change here.

## 3. Hiding more than usual

A cat that suddenly spends all day under the bed is telling you something changed — a new pet, a move, even rearranged furniture.

## 4. Appetite swings

Eating much less, or suddenly demanding food constantly, can both be stress responses worth noting.

## 5. Less play, more tension

Reduced play, a tucked posture, or flinching at normal sounds all point to a cat that doesn't feel safe.

## What helps

Give your cat **vertical space** to climb, predictable routines, and enough resources — the rule of thumb is one litter box per cat, plus one extra. If signs persist, book a check-up; ruling out pain always comes first.
