The first year shapes your dog's health and temperament for life. Here's a month-by-month map so nothing slips through the cracks.

## Weeks 6–16: the foundation

This is the busiest stretch. Your puppy needs a **course of vaccinations** spaced a few weeks apart, plus deworming. It's also the critical **socialization window** — gentle, positive exposure to new people, surfaces, and sounds now prevents fear later.

## Months 4–6: habits and health

- Start (or continue) **parasite prevention** for fleas, ticks, and worms.
- Talk to your vet about the timing of **spay/neuter** for your breed and size.
- Lock in **house-training and basic cues** — consistency beats intensity.

## Months 6–12: growing up

Adult teeth come in, so provide safe chew options. Transition from puppy food to an adult diet at the right time for your dog's size — large breeds grow more slowly and shouldn't switch too early.

## Don't skip the boring stuff

Get your puppy comfortable being handled — paws, ears, mouth — early. It makes nail trims, dental care, and vet visits dramatically easier for the next 15 years.

## Your first-visit checklist

Bring any paperwork from the breeder or shelter, a fresh stool sample if you can, and a list of questions. There's no such thing as a silly one in that first appointment.
