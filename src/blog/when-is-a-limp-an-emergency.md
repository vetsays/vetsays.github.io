A sudden limp is alarming, but not every one needs an emergency visit. Here's how I help owners decide.

## Call the vet right away if you see…

- The leg is **dangling, swollen, or at an odd angle** — a possible fracture or dislocation.
- Your pet **won't bear any weight** at all on the limb.
- There's an open wound, heavy bleeding, or a clearly painful, hot joint.
- The limp follows a **major trauma** like a car accident or a fall from height.

These deserve same-day attention.

## It's usually okay to watch for 24–48 hours if…

Your pet is still using the leg, eating normally, and acting like themselves, and the limp is mild. Rest them — short leash walks only, no jumping or rough play — and see if it eases.

## How to rest the right way

Strict rest means genuinely limiting movement, not just "taking it easy." Skip the dog park, block access to stairs and furniture, and keep walks short and slow.

## When "wait" becomes "go"

If a mild limp hasn't improved in two days, gets worse, or your pet seems painful, book a visit. Limping is a symptom, not a diagnosis — and catching the cause early almost always makes treatment simpler.
